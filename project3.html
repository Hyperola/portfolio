Hoodit

UI/UX Design & Front-end Development

Overview

Hoodit is a student-focused rental and marketplace web app. It connects students with verified landlords and sellers, offering secure housing and a trusted space to buy/sell essentials.

User Research

Method: Surveys, interviews, and community observation.

Key Insights:

  • Students struggle to find affordable, verified listings.
  • Fear of scams makes them wary of online transactions.
  • There's a demand for a student-only trusted platform.

Problem Statement

Students need a safe, reliable way to find housing and buy/sell goods within their campus communities.

Design Process

Personas: Developed 3 personas: "Busy Student," "Freelance Creative," and "Tech-Savvy Professional."

Wireframes: Sketched low-fidelity wireframes in Figma, focusing on a tabbed interface for quick toggling.

Prototyping: Built high-fidelity prototypes, tested with 12 users, and iterated based on feedback—e.g., simplified the onboarding flow.

Visual Design: Used Slate Gray as the base with orange accents (#F28C38) for vibrancy, ensuring a modern, approachable feel.

Development Process

Tech Stack: React.js, Node.js, MongoDB, Express.js, Google Auth, Vercel.

Features:

  • Verified house listings and seller profiles.
  • Secure Login (Google).
  • Real-time chat & Map-based Filtering.
  • Wishlist & Favorites.
  • User reviews & ratings.

Challenges

Challenge: Designing a seamless user experience that combines house listings and marketplace without overwhelming users.

Solution: Simplified navigation by separating both features into clear categories, used intuitive icons, consistent color schemes, and clear CTAs to guide users. Streamlined user flows and focused on minimal layouts to ensure clarity and ease of use.
