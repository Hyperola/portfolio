The Hub

UI/UX Design & Front-end Development

Overview

The Hub is a mobile app designed to centralize social networking, productivity tools, and content discovery into one sleek platform. My role was to design the UI/UX and develop the front-end using React.

User Research

Method: Conducted 20 user interviews and surveyed 50 participants aged 18-35.

Findings: Users felt overwhelmed by juggling multiple apps for social, work, and media needs. They craved a unified experience with fast navigation and personalization.

Problem Statement

How might we create a single app that integrates social networking, task management, and curated content without sacrificing simplicity or speed?

Design Process

Personas: Developed 3 personas: "Busy Student," "Freelance Creative," and "Tech-Savvy Professional."

Wireframes: Sketched low-fidelity wireframes in Figma, focusing on a tabbed interface for quick toggling.

Prototyping: Built high-fidelity prototypes, tested with 12 users, and iterated based on feedback—e.g., simplified the onboarding flow.

Visual Design: Used Slate Gray as the base with orange accents (#F28C38) for vibrancy, ensuring a modern, approachable feel.

Development

Tech Stack: React, CSS Modules, React Router.

Features: Implemented a responsive layout, smooth tab transitions, and a custom infinite scroll for content feeds.

Challenges

Challenge: Balancing feature density with a clean UI.

Solution: Introduced collapsible menus and prioritized content hierarchy via card-based design.

Results

Metrics: Post-launch, users reported a 40% reduction in app-switching time. Engagement rose by 28% due to personalized feeds.

Testimonial: "The Hub feels like my digital home—everything I need in one place!" - Beta User
